## 🎤 Stage Presence Notes

### Voice & pacing
- **Preksha** opens: warm, slower, storytelling. The hook has to land in the first 10 seconds.
- **Dhun** continues: more energetic, technical, demo-driven. Keep the energy up through the demos.
- **Both** close: confident, concise.

### What to AVOID
- Don't read the screen. The audience can see it.
- Don't say "um", "so basically", "yeah". Pause instead.
- Don't apologize for the site loading. If a page lags, say "while this loads" and continue talking about the previous point.
- Don't go over 6 minutes. The timer is your enemy.

### What TO do
- **Make eye contact with the judges** when making key points.
- **Gesture toward the screen** with an open palm, not pointing.
- **Smile on the "73% learn more from YouTube than school" line** — it's the punchline.
- **Pause after the AI generates a response.** Let the silence sell it.

### If something breaks
- **Internet down:** Preksha has the project open on her laptop as a backup. Switch to her.
- **Gemini rate limit:** The script has fallback data on every endpoint. Just keep talking — the response is already there, you just don't say "live".
- **Wrong page loaded:** Anuj navigates back; don't acknowledge the mistake.
